Conclusion: Key Factors in Titanic Survival

Based on the comprehensive analysis of the Titanic dataset, the most important factors influencing survival were:

  • Gender was the single most important factor, with women having a 74.2% survival rate compared to just 18.9% for men. This was due to the "women and children first" protocol followed during the evacuation.
  • Passenger class significantly impacted survival, with 1st class passengers having a 62.9% survival rate compared to 24.2% for 3rd class. This was due to better location on the ship and priority access to lifeboats.
  • Age played a role as children had priority to lifeboats, resulting in a 59.0% survival rate for children compared to 38.3% for adults.
  • Family size influenced survival, with small families (1-3 members) having better chances than those traveling alone or with very large families.

    • These factors demonstrate how societal norms and structural inequalities during the disaster significantly influenced survival outcomes. The "women and children first" protocol was clearly followed, but access to lifeboats was also strongly influenced by socioeconomic status as reflected in passenger class.
